# LinkedIn Article Strategy

**Project:** Faintech Lab - AMC MVP Launch
**Status:** Ready for execution upon CEO approval
**Created:** 2026-03-27

---

## Platform Characteristics

- **Audience:** Technical leaders, enterprise decision-makers, B2B SaaS founders
- **Behavior:** Values credibility, case studies, technical depth
- **Timing:** 08:00-10:00 local time, 17:00-19:00 local time
- **Engagement:** Comments more valuable than likes for quality signal

---

## Article Structure (800-1200 words)

### Headline
**"After 687 HackerNews posts about AI agents forgetting context, we built a persistent memory system to fix it"**

### Subheadline
**Technical breakdown of how we built Faintech Lab, why it matters for enterprise AI workflows, and what we learned from beta launch**

---

## Sections

### 1. The Hidden Problem (150 words)
- Start with data point: 687 HN posts in 6 months
- Enterprise AI workflow paralysis: Context repetition costs
- Real example from beta user (anonymized)

### 2. Our Solution (200 words)
- Faintech Lab architecture overview
- Byte-rover memory graph: Not just RAG
- Persistent context across sessions, not just within one conversation

### 3. Technical Deep Dive (300 words)
- Stack decisions: Next.js + TypeScript + Neon
- Why not vector-only: We do graph + structured data
- Tradeoffs we made (latency vs recall, storage vs compute)
- Link to GitHub for curious engineers

### 4. Enterprise Use Cases (200 words)
- Developer onboarding: New hire ramp-up time reduction
- Technical debt: Consistent explanations across team
- Customer support: Context retention for multi-turn issues

### 5. Launch Learnings (150 words)
- Post-launch Day 3: Platform healthy (100% tests)
- Early feedback patterns (from beta)
- What we got right vs wrong

### 6. Open Source Philosophy (100 words)
- Why open source: Transparency builds trust
- Community contribution guidelines
- Future roadmap co-creation

### 7. Call to Action (100 words)
- Beta signup link
- Enterprise outreach: DM for pilot
- Ask: "What's your biggest AI workflow friction?"

---

## A/B Test Hypotheses

**Hypothesis 1:** Technical deep dive headline converts better than problem statement
**Hypothesis 2:** Enterprise use cases section increases B2B signups vs technical sections
**Hypothesis 3:** "Launch learnings" section builds more trust than "Open source philosophy"
**Hypothesis 4:** Explicit CTA at bottom vs embedded throughout increases conversion

**Success Metrics:**
- Article views
- Scroll depth (80%+ indicates engagement)
- Signups from LinkedIn referrer
- LinkedIn connection requests (signal of interest)
- Comments (quality > quantity)

*"Good point - [Competitor] excels at [their focus]. Our approach focuses on persistent memory as the missing piece in AI workflows. Many teams use both - happy to share integration notes."*

*"Appreciate the feedback! This is exactly why we launched in beta. I've logged this for our roadmap team. You can track progress at [public roadmap link]."*
