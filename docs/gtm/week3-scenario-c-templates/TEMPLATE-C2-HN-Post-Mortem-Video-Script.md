## Video Structure

**Total Duration:** 2-3 minutes

**Style:** Loom-style walkthrough or founder talk

## Opening Hook (0:00-0:15)

**Visual:** Founder face-to-camera or "What went wrong" title card

**Script:**
> "We launched on Hacker News yesterday with a completely broken product.
> 
> Here's what happened, why it happened, and what we learned."

---

## Section 1: What Happened (0:15-0:45)

**Visual:** Split screen - show:
- Left: HN post (Show HN: [Product Name])
- Right: Terminal with `curl https://api.example.com/health` returning 404

**Script:**
> "We launched at 17:00 EET on April 1st.
> 
> The frontend was live - you could visit the site, see the landing page, click 'Sign Up'.
> 
> But when you clicked sign up... nothing happened.
> 
> The backend API was completely missing. All API endpoints returned 404."

---

## Section 2: Root Cause Analysis (0:45-1:30)

**Visual:** Architecture diagram animation showing:
- Frontend deployed to Vercel
- Backend FastAPI app... not deployed
- Red X between them

**Script:**
> "So what went wrong?
> 
> Two things: technical gap and process failure.
> 
> Technical gap: Our deployment pipeline checked out frontend code, but never deployed the backend FastAPI app. The CI pipeline passed, but the actual backend never went to production.
> 
> Process failure: We ran our launch checklist, but it didn't include 'verify backend is live'. We verified the frontend (HTTP 200), checked the demo URL, confirmed PRs were merged... but never actually tested the signup flow end-to-end."

---

## Section 3: Why This Matters (1:30-2:00)

**Visual:** Show:
- Timeline: Backend identified at 16:37 → Launched at 17:00 → Still not deployed at 18:01
- Escalation screenshot to c-suite-chat

**Script:**
> "Here's the thing: we knew the backend wasn't deployed 23 minutes before launch.
> 
> A monitoring check at 16:37 EET flagged it. We escalated to the team.
> 
> But we launched anyway.
> 
> That's the process failure. No auto-halt on critical blocker. No automated health check that would stop the launch gate. No single point of ownership for 'stop the line' authority."

---

## Section 4: What We're Fixing (2:00-2:30)

**Visual:** Bulleted list of fixes, each appearing as spoken:

**Script:**
> "So what are we fixing?
> 
> Launch gate checklist: Backend health check is now a mandatory step. No launch without `curl /api/health` returning 200.
> 
> Auto-halt: If any critical check fails within 2 hours of launch time, auto-halt the launch and page the CTO.
> 
> Health monitoring: We're adding automated health checks every 10 minutes. If backend goes down, we know before users do.
> 
> Transparency policy: From now on, we share deployment status publicly. If we have issues, you'll see them on our status page, not discover them when you try to sign up."

---

## Section 5: What's Next (2:30-2:45)

**Visual:** Timeline graphic:
- Now: Backend deployment in progress
- Tomorrow: Re-launch
- Week 3: Full GTM execution

**Script:**
> "The backend is deploying now. We'll be back live tomorrow.
> 
> We're running Week 3 GTM next week - Reddit, LinkedIn, syndication.
> 
> This time, with a working product and better launch gates."

---

## Closing Call-to-Action (2:45-3:00)

**Visual:** Founder face-to-camera

**Script:**
> "We learned a lot from this. Engineering culture isn't about never failing - it's about failing fast, learning publicly, and fixing the root cause.
> 
> If you've run into deployment gaps or launch failures, I'd love to hear your launch gate setups.
> 
> Let's discuss in the comments."

## Production Notes

### Filming Guidelines

**Format Options:**
1. **Loom-style walkthrough:** Screen-share the timeline, terminal, architecture diagram while narrating
2. **Founder talk:** Face-to-camera with title cards and text overlays
3. **Hybrid:** Founder intro → screen-share technical details → founder conclusion

**Visual Assets:**
- HN post screenshot (Show HN title)
- Terminal output showing 404 errors
- Deployment architecture diagram (use Excalidraw or Figma)
- Timeline graphic showing escalation duration
- Launch gate checklist screenshot

**Audio:**
- Clean audio, no background noise
- Speak at normal conversational pace (130-150 words/minute)
- Subtitles auto-generated by YouTube for accessibility

### Call-to-Action Strategy

**Discussion Starters:**
- "What launch gate checks do you use before hitting publish?"
- "Ever launched with a broken backend? How do you prevent it now?"
- "Automated health checks: what tools do you recommend?"

**Engagement Strategy:**
- Reply to every top comment within 1 hour
- Share technical details (deployment commands, architecture)
- Link to Week 3 Scenario C HN post-mortem document (TEMPLATE-C1)
- Offer to share our launch gate checklist if helpful

### Distribution

**Primary Channels:**
1. **YouTube:** Upload video, embed in HN post
2. **Hacker News:** "Show HN" post with video embed and TEMPLATE-C1 text
3. **LinkedIn:** Post video with "Engineering Culture Transparency" tagline

**Timing:**
- YouTube upload: April 13 (Day 1 of Week 3)
- HN post: April 13 (Day 1, coordinated with video)
- LinkedIn: April 14 (Day 2)

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ **DO NOT:**
- Pitch the product in the video
- Blame individual team members (focus on process, not people)
- Make excuses about "we were tired" or "it was a busy day"
- Hide technical details (show the commands, the errors, the architecture)
- Delete negative comments (address them publicly with data)

✅ **DO:**
- Take full responsibility as a company
- Show technical specifics (curl commands, exact times, error codes)
- Demonstrate learning (what changed, what won't happen again)
- Invite discussion about launch gates and monitoring
- Focus on engineering culture, not product promotion
