## Email Template Structure

### Subject Line Options (A/B Test):
```
Option A: Research Partnership: Joint Agent Memory Architecture Research - Stanford/Faintech
Option B: Collaboration Invitation: Advancing Agent Memory Research Together - Stanford/Faintech
Option C: Research Opportunity: Production-Ready Memory Architecture for Your Lab - Stanford/Faintech
```

### Salutation (Personalized):
```
Dear Professor [Last Name],
```

### Opening Paragraph (Establish Connection):
```
I'm [Your Name] from Faintech, and I've been following your lab's groundbreaking work on [Specific Research Area, e.g., "agent memory architectures" or "multi-agent coordination"]. Your recent paper "[Specific Paper Title]" particularly resonated with our research direction at Faintech.

We've developed AMC (Agent Memory Core), a production-ready memory architecture for autonomous AI agents, and we believe a research partnership between our teams could significantly advance the field of agent memory research.
```

### Value Proposition Paragraph (What's in it for them):
```
AMC offers a unique research opportunity for your lab:

🧠 **Production-Ready Architecture:** Access to a battle-tested memory system with 99.3% reliability (149/150 test cases passing), providing real-world validation for memory research hypotheses.

📊 **Rich Research Data:** Anonymized memory traces and patterns from our deployment, offering unprecedented insights into how autonomous agents use and maintain memory over extended periods.

🤝 **Joint Research Potential:** Co-authorship opportunities on papers about agent memory scaling, cross-agent learning, and practical memory compression techniques - exactly the areas where your lab excels.

🔬 **Technical Resources:** Full access to AMC's research instance with complete API, analytics dashboard, and engineering support for integration into your existing research workflows.
```

### Partnership Structure Paragraph (Low-commitment, High-value):
```
We're proposing a light-weight research collaboration with no commercial obligations:

🔬 **Phase 1 (Immediate):** Joint research exploration with access to AMC research instance
📝 **Phase 2 (3-6 months):** Collaborative research papers and shared datasets
🚀 **Phase 3 (Future):** Optional technical integration and commercial pathways

Importantly, this requires:
- No financial commitment from your lab
- No exclusivity or IP transfer restrictions
- Research-paced timeline (not commercial deadlines)
- Full academic freedom to publish findings
```

### Specific Lab Alignment Paragraph (Highly personalized):
```
Your lab's work on [Specific Research Area] aligns perfectly with AMC's capabilities. For example:

[Lab-Specific Connection 1]: Your research on [Specific Paper/Topic] directly relates to AMC's [Specific Feature/Capability].

[Lab-Specific Connection 2]: We believe our memory compression techniques could enhance your work on [Their Research Area], while your expertise in [Their Expertise] could help us improve AMC's [Specific Area].

[Lab-Specific Connection 3]: The joint publication potential in [Specific Conference/Area] would be significant for both our teams.
```

### Call to Action (Clear Next Steps):
```
I'd like to schedule a 30-minute technical discussion between our research teams to explore this collaboration further. During this call, we can:

• Demonstrate AMC's capabilities and research value
• Discuss your lab's specific research interests and how AMC could help
• Outline potential joint research projects and publication opportunities
• Answer any technical questions about integration and data access

**Timeline Note:** We're activating Phase 1 research partnerships immediately, with a 24-hour partnership window closing April 6, 08:48 EEST. I'd appreciate the opportunity to connect this week to ensure your lab is included in this initial research cohort.

Would you be available for a brief call this week? I'm flexible around your schedule and can arrange a meeting at your convenience.
```

### Closing (Professional but warm):
```
Thank you for considering this research collaboration opportunity. I believe our teams could make significant contributions to the field of agent memory research together.

I look forward to the possibility of working with you.

Best regards,

[Your Name]
Partnerships Lead
Faintech Solutions SRL

📧 partnerships@faintech.com
🔗 amc.faintech.com/research (Partnership One-Pager)
📱 +40 [Phone Number - if applicable]
```

2. **Technical-Focused:** Highlight AMC's technical capabilities and API access
3. **Collaboration-Focused:** Stress the low-commitment, high-value partnership structure

### Subject Line Testing:
- **Research-oriented:** "Joint Research Opportunity: Agent Memory Architecture Collaboration"
- **Technical-oriented:** "Technical Access: Production-Ready Memory Architecture for Research"
- **Collaboration-oriented:** "Partnership Invitation: Advancing Agent Memory Research Together"

### Tracking Method:
- Use different subject lines for tracking response rates
- Include specific UTM parameters in links if sending digitally
- Track which labs respond to which approaches

## Email Best Practices

### Timing:
- **Send Time:** Tuesday-Thursday, 10:00-14:00 recipient's local time
- **Follow-up:** 48 hours after initial email
- **Final Notice:** 24 hours before deadline

### Personalization:
- **Research Specific:** Reference specific papers and research areas
- **Lab Specific:** Mention the lab's unique strengths and contributions
- **Person Specific:** Address the professor's specific expertise and interests

### Technical Details:
- **Attach:** AMC Research Partnership One-Pager
- **Link:** amc.faintech.com/research (if available)
- **Contact:** partnerships@faintech.com for technical questions

### Professional Tone:
- **Academic:** Use research-appropriate language and terminology
- **Respectful:** Acknowledge their expertise and research contributions
- **Collaborative:** Emphasize mutual benefits and shared goals

## Response Tracking

### Create tracking spreadsheet with columns:
- **Lab Name:** Institution and lab name
- **Contact Person:** Professor or researcher contacted
- **Email Sent:** Date/time of initial email
- **Email Variant:** Which subject line and approach used
- **Response Received:** Date/time of response
- **Response Type:** Positive, negative, request for more info
- **Meeting Scheduled:** Date/time of research call
- **Partnership Status:** Prospect, Interested, Committed, Not Interested

### Success Metrics:
- **Response Rate:** Target 40% (4 out of 10 labs respond)
- **Meeting Rate:** Target 25% (2-3 labs schedule calls)
- **Partnership Rate:** Target 20% (2 research partnerships secured)
