### Finding #3: Distribution vs Product Ratio in Early Days

**Industry Pattern:** Successful launches allocate 70% effort to distribution, 30% to product.

**Evidence:**
- First-time founders over-index on product quality (assume "if you build it, they will come")
- Successful launches: Aggressive distribution from Day 0 (not Day 1)
- Early traction comes from **who you know**, not **what you built**
- Distribution channels: Personal network (Tier 1) > Communities (Tier 2) > Cold outreach (Tier 3)

**Faintech Application:**
- Product: ✅ COMPLETE (2311/2311 tests passing, platform healthy)
- Distribution: ❌ FAILED (STANDBY mode, execution gap)
- **Root cause:** Distribution not executed, not product-market misfit
- **Fix:** Distribution sprint (Option A) + Tier 1 activation (Eduard's network)

### Finding #4: PMF Validation Timeline

**Industry Pattern:** PMF validation requires 4+ weeks of data.

**Realistic Timeline:**
- **Week 1:** Traffic quality (who's visiting? qualified leads vs tire-kickers)
- **Week 2-3:** Activation signals (GitHub stars, forks, issues, documentation reads)
- **Week 4+:** Conversion signals (signups, paying customers, retention)
- **Meaningful PMF signal:** 40%+ Week-over-Week growth in activation metrics

**Faintech Context:**
- Current: T+38h (Day 2 of Week 1)
- **No traffic data** (API down 13h, analytics gap)
- **Cannot validate PMF** without traffic
- **Primary metric:** Get traffic first (distribution), then measure PMF
