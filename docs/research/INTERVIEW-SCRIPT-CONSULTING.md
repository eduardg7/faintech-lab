# Customer Interview Script — Consulting Firms Segment

**Research Date:** 2026-03-16
**Researcher:** sales (growth & marketing lead)
**Sprint:** Sprint 2
**Target Segment:** Consulting Firms (Romania/EU)
**Goal:** Validate hypothesis that consulting firms will pay €800-€1,200/month for AI-powered practice management

---

## Interview Overview
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Target:** Consulting firm partners/principals (IT, HR, management, marketing, legal)
**Sample Size:** 3-5 interviews
**Context:** Customer validation for Faintech New-Product Track

---

## Opening and Setup (1-2 minutes)

**Opening:**
"Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm researching how consulting firms manage their operations and I'd like to learn about your experience.

This conversation will take about 30 minutes. I'll ask about your current workflows, challenges, and what would improve your practice. There are no right or wrong answers — I'm here to learn from your experience.

Everything you share will be kept confidential and used only for research purposes. Do you have any questions before we begin?"

**Context setting:**
"Can you tell me briefly about your consulting practice — what type of consulting you do, how many consultants you have, and what types of clients you serve?"

---

## Question 1: Multi-Client Time Management (8-10 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pain point: "Consultants juggling 3-5 concurrent projects across different clients, leading to missed deadlines and context switching overhead"

**Primary questions:**
1. "How many client projects do you or your consultants typically manage concurrently?"
2. "How do you handle scheduling and deadline management across multiple clients?"
3. "Can you walk me through a typical day or week — how do you prioritize which client work to focus on?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "Have you ever missed a deadline or felt overwhelmed by juggling multiple projects?"
- "How much time do you spend switching between different client contexts in a typical week?"
- "What tools or processes do you use to track deadlines and manage priorities?"

**What to listen for:**
- Frequency of missed deadlines or urgent firefights
- Time spent on context switching (if they say "15-30%" or "several hours a week" → strong signal)
- Manual processes (spreadsheets, calendars, email reminders) vs. automated tools
- Frustration or stress when describing scheduling challenges

---

## Question 2: Knowledge Silos Between Projects (8-10 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pain point: "No centralized knowledge base across client engagements, learnings don't transfer"

**Primary questions:**
1. "When a consultant completes a project, what happens to the learnings, solutions, or approaches they developed?"
2. "How do you share best practices or successful solutions across different client projects?"
3. "When a new consultant joins a similar project to one you've done before, how do they get up to speed?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "Can you think of a time when a mistake was repeated because knowledge wasn't shared?"
- "How do you track what worked well on past projects?"
- "What would happen if you needed to find a solution from a project completed 6 months ago?"

**What to listen for:**
- Informal or ad-hoc knowledge sharing ("we talk in meetings" or "it's in people's heads")
- No centralized repository or documentation system
- Frustration about repeating work or reinventing the wheel
- Stories of missed opportunities because past learnings weren't available

---

## Question 3: Manual Client Coordination (6-8 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pain point: "Owner must manually coordinate between consultants, clients, and delivery teams"

**Primary questions:**
1. "How do you manage communication and coordination between consultants, clients, and any delivery teams?"
2. "How do you handle client status updates, progress reports, or check-ins?"
3. "What's your process for managing client expectations around deliverables and timelines?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "How much of your time is spent on coordination vs. actual consulting work?"
- "Have you had miscommunications or missed client expectations because coordination was manual?"
- "What would improve your client coordination process?"

**What to listen for:**
- High coordination overhead (10-20% of billable time)
- Manual processes (email threads, WhatsApp, ad-hoc meetings)
- Stories of miscommunication or client dissatisfaction
- Desire for automation (if they say "I wish this was automatic" → strong signal)

---

## Question 4: Inconsistent Deliverable Tracking (6-8 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pain point: "Different consultants use different templates, no standardized QA process"

**Primary questions:**
1. "How do you standardize deliverables (proposals, reports, presentations) across different consultants?"
2. "What's your quality assurance process for client deliverables?"
3. "How do you ensure consistent quality across different projects?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "Have you had to rework deliverables because quality was inconsistent?"
- "How do you track which templates or approaches work best?"
- "What would help you standardize and improve deliverable quality?"

**What to listen for:**
- No standardized templates or QA process
- Variability in quality ("it depends on who worked on it")
- Rework or client feedback about inconsistent deliverables
- Interest in standardization tools

---

## Question 5: Billing and Pricing (5-7 minutes)

**Goal:** Assess willingness to pay for a solution

**Primary questions:**
1. "If there was a tool that solved your biggest time management and coordination challenges — automating scheduling, centralizing knowledge, and improving client coordination — how much would you be willing to pay per month?"
2. "What's your current monthly spend on practice management tools (project management, CRM, billing software, etc.)?"
3. "How much time or revenue would you save if your biggest coordination challenges were solved?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "What's the most you'd be willing to pay for a solution that saved you 10-15 hours per week on coordination?"
- "Would you prefer a monthly subscription or a per-user pricing model?"
- "What would you expect in return for paying €800-€1,200/month for a practice management solution?"

**What to listen for:**
- Willingness to pay €800-€1,200/month (if yes → strong validation signal)
- Current tool spend (if already spending €500+/month → easier to justify Faintech cost)
- Clear ROI calculation (time saved × billable rate = revenue gained)
- Hesitation or pushback on price (ask what would make it worth it)

---

## Closing (2-3 minutes)

**Summary:**
"Thank you for sharing your experience. Based on our conversation, I heard that [repeat 2-3 key pain points they mentioned] are your biggest challenges. Is that accurate?"

**Follow-up:**
"If we build a solution that addresses these challenges, would you be willing to test it when it's ready?"

**Next steps:**
"Is there anyone else you'd recommend I speak with about their consulting practice experience?"

**Closing:**
"Thank you again for your time. I'll send you a summary of our conversation and keep you updated on our progress. Do you have any questions for me?"

---

## Hypothesis Validation Criteria

**PASS Criteria (must meet all 3):**
1. **3/5 interviews confirm time management/knowledge silos as top pain** (identified in Q1 and Q2)
2. **2/3 express willingness to pay >€800/month** (identified in Q5)
3. **Clear ROI articulated** (interviewee can calculate: time saved × billable rate > subscription cost)

**FAIL Criteria (any of these):**
1. **<3/5 interviews confirm time management/knowledge silos as top pain** (other issues are more urgent)
2. **<2/3 express willingness to pay >€800/month** (budget ceiling is lower)
3. **Unclear or weak ROI** (interviewee can't articulate time savings or revenue impact)

**INCONCLUSIVE Criteria:**
- 2/5 interviews confirm pain point (borderline)
- 2/3 express willingness to pay exactly €800/month (borderline)
- Mixed feedback on ROI (some see value, others don't)

**Action if INCONCLUSIVE:** Interview 2-3 more firms to reach stronger signal

## Vanta's Framework Applied

**Christina Cacioppo's 75% Prediction Heuristic:**
- Continue interviewing until we can accurately predict what the next consulting firm will say about their time management and knowledge silo challenges
- If we can predict 75% of responses before the interview, we've achieved understanding

**Business Outcome Alignment:**
- Look for business outcome alignment: are they trying to increase billable hours, reduce non-billable time, or improve client retention?
- Position Faintech as solving that business outcome (e.g., "save 10-15 hours/week of non-billable time" = increase revenue by €2,000-€5,000/month at €200/hour billable rate)

**Ask to See Calendar (if possible):**
- "Can you show me how you manage your calendar across multiple clients?" → reveals actual workflow complexity
- Look for: manual scheduling tools, conflict resolution process, deadline tracking method
