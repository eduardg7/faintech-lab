# Customer Interview Script — Marketing Agencies Segment

**Research Date:** 2026-03-16
**Researcher:** sales (growth & marketing lead)
**Sprint:** Sprint 2
**Target Segment:** Marketing Agencies (Romania/EU)
**Goal:** Validate hypothesis that marketing agencies will pay €600-€800/month for AI-powered Marketing OS

---

## Interview Overview
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Target:** Marketing agency founders/directors (digital, social media, PR, advertising)
**Sample Size:** 3-5 interviews
**Context:** Customer validation for Faintech New-Product Track

---

## Opening and Setup (1-2 minutes)

**Opening:**
"Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm researching how marketing agencies manage their operations and I'd like to learn about your experience.

This conversation will take about 30 minutes. I'll ask about your current workflows, challenges, and what would improve your agency. There are no right or wrong answers — I'm here to learn from your experience.

Everything you share will be kept confidential and used only for research purposes. Do you have any questions before we begin?"

**Context setting:**
"Can you tell me briefly about your marketing agency — what type of marketing you do, how many clients you manage, and what channels you work with (social media, ads, PR, email)?"

---

## Question 1: Campaign Coordination Complexity (8-10 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pain point: "Multiple concurrent campaigns across different channels, leading to channel conflicts, message inconsistencies, and budget waste"

**Primary questions:**
1. "How many concurrent marketing campaigns do you typically manage for different clients?"
2. "How do you coordinate campaigns across different channels (social media, ads, PR, email)?"
3. "Can you walk me through a typical campaign launch — how do you ensure messaging is consistent across all channels?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "Have you experienced channel conflicts or inconsistent messaging when running multi-channel campaigns?"
- "How much time do you spend on campaign coordination in a typical week?"
- "What tools or processes do you use to manage campaign schedules and messaging?"
- "Have you estimated how much budget waste occurs due to poor channel coordination?"

**What to listen for:**
- Complexity of managing 5-10+ concurrent campaigns across multiple channels
- Manual processes for coordination (spreadsheets, calendars, ad-hoc meetings)
- Stories of channel conflicts or message inconsistencies
- Time spent on coordination (if 5-10 hours/week → strong signal)
- Budget waste estimates (15-25% spend efficiency loss)

---

## Question 2: Content Production Bottlenecks (8-10 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pain point: "Agency depends on external suppliers for creative assets, causing production delays"

**Primary questions:**
1. "How do you handle content production (copywriting, design, video, social media assets)?"
2. "Do you produce content in-house or work with external suppliers (freelancers, agencies)?"
3. "How do you manage content production timelines for campaign launches?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "How often do production delays impact your campaign launch schedules?"
- "How long does it typically take to get creative assets from external suppliers?"
- "What happens when a campaign needs quick creative turnaround?"
- "How do you handle quality control when working with multiple suppliers?"

**What to listen for:**
- Dependence on external suppliers for creative assets
- Production delays (3-7 days) impacting campaign timelines
- Stories of rushed quality or late campaign launches due to content bottlenecks
- Frustration with supplier coordination and timelines
- Interest in AI content generation (if they say "I wish we could generate content faster" → strong signal)

---

## Question 3: Performance Fragmentation (6-8 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pain point: "KPIs scattered across multiple platforms, causing manual data aggregation and reporting latency"

**Primary questions:**
1. "How do you track performance across different marketing channels (Google Ads, Meta Ads, website analytics, email)?"
2. "How do you create client reports — do you aggregate data manually or use a unified dashboard?"
3. "How often do you analyze performance and optimize campaigns?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "How much time do you spend aggregating data for client reports in a typical month?"
- "How quickly can you identify performance issues or optimization opportunities?"
- "What tools do you use for analytics and reporting?"
- "Have you experienced delayed optimization because data was scattered across platforms?"

**What to listen for:**
- Manual data aggregation across multiple platforms (5-10 hours/month)
- No unified analytics dashboard (switching between Google Ads, Meta Ads, GA4, etc.)
- Reporting latency (1-2 weeks before performance is analyzed)
- Stories of missed optimization opportunities due to data fragmentation
- Desire for unified performance view (if they say "I wish all my data was in one place" → strong signal)

---

## Question 4: Client Communication Overhead (6-8 minutes)

**Goal:** Validate pain point: "Clients expect frequent updates but agencies struggle to provide detailed reports consistently"

**Primary questions:**
1. "How do you handle client communication and status updates for ongoing campaigns?"
2. "How often do clients expect performance reports or updates?"
3. "What's your process for creating client reports?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "How much time do you spend on client communication and reporting in a typical week?"
- "Have you had clients express dissatisfaction with reporting frequency or detail?"
- "How do you handle client requests for ad-hoc updates or urgent information?"
- "What would improve your client communication workflow?"

**What to listen for:**
- High client communication overhead (10-20% of agency time)
- Manual report creation (copy-pasting from multiple platforms, formatting in slides)
- Client churn stories related to communication issues
- Frustration with manual reporting workflows
- Interest in automated report generation (if they say "I wish reports were automatic" → strong signal)

---

## Question 5: Budget Optimization Challenges (5-7 minutes)

**Goal:** Assess willingness to pay for a solution

**Primary questions:**
1. "How do you optimize ad spend and bids across different campaigns and channels?"
2. "How quickly can you identify overspending or underperforming campaigns?"
3. "If there was a tool that solved your biggest campaign coordination and performance challenges — automating multi-channel orchestration, unifying analytics, and optimizing budget in real-time — how much would you be willing to pay per month?"
4. "What's your current monthly spend on marketing tools (analytics platforms, reporting software, ad management tools)?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "What's the most you'd be willing to pay for a solution that saved you 10-15 hours per week on coordination and reporting?"
- "Would you prefer a monthly subscription or a per-client pricing model?"
- "What would you expect in return for paying €600-€800/month for a Marketing OS solution?"
- "How would you justify this cost to your clients or business partners?"

**What to listen for:**
- Willingness to pay €600-€800/month (if yes → strong validation signal)
- Current tool spend (if already spending €400+/month → easier to justify Faintech cost)
- Clear ROI calculation (time saved × billable rate = revenue gained)
- Hesitation or pushback on price (ask what would make it worth it)

---

## Closing (2-3 minutes)

**Summary:**
"Thank you for sharing your experience. Based on our conversation, I heard that [repeat 2-3 key pain points they mentioned] are your biggest challenges. Is that accurate?"

**Follow-up:**
"If we build a solution that addresses these challenges, would you be willing to test it when it's ready?"

**Next steps:**
"Is there anyone else you'd recommend I speak with about their agency operations experience?"

**Closing:**
"Thank you again for your time. I'll send you a summary of our conversation and keep you updated on our progress. Do you have any questions for me?"

---

## Hypothesis Validation Criteria

**PASS Criteria (must meet all 3):**
1. **3/5 interviews confirm campaign coordination/performance fragmentation as top pain** (identified in Q1 and Q3)
2. **2/3 express willingness to pay >€600/month** (identified in Q5)
3. **Clear ROI articulated** (interviewee can calculate: time saved × billable rate > subscription cost)

**FAIL Criteria (any of these):**
1. **<3/5 interviews confirm campaign coordination/performance fragmentation as top pain** (other issues are more urgent)
2. **<2/3 express willingness to pay >€600/month** (budget ceiling is lower)
3. **Unclear or weak ROI** (interviewee can't articulate time savings or revenue impact)

**INCONCLUSIVE Criteria:**
- 2/5 interviews confirm pain point (borderline)
- 2/3 express willingness to pay exactly €600/month (borderline)
- Mixed feedback on ROI (some see value, others don't)

**Action if INCONCLUSIVE:** Interview 2-3 more agencies to reach stronger signal

## Vanta's Framework Applied

**Christina Cacioppo's 75% Prediction Heuristic:**
- Continue interviewing until we can accurately predict what the next marketing agency will say about their campaign coordination and performance fragmentation challenges
- If we can predict 75% of responses before the interview, we've achieved understanding

**Business Outcome Alignment:**
- Look for business outcome alignment: are they trying to increase client retention, improve campaign ROI, reduce non-billable time, or scale agency growth?
- Position Faintech as solving that business outcome (e.g., "save 10-15 hours/week of non-billable time" = increase capacity for more clients at same staffing)
- For agencies: focus on revenue impact (more clients, higher retainers, better campaign ROI)

**Ask to See Current Workflow (if possible):**
- "Can you show me how you manage campaign schedules across different channels?" → reveals actual workflow complexity
- "Can you show me your client reporting process?" → reveals manual aggregation steps
- Look for: manual tools, data entry steps, switching between multiple platforms
